# Why Frontier? A Smarter Way to Use Your AI Agent

You already have access to a powerful AI agent. The question isn't *what* it can do — it's *how* you experience it. Both Frontier and Open WebUI connect you to the same underlying agent. The difference is everything around it.

Here's why Frontier is the better choice for getting real work done.

---

## Your Agent, Not a Model Picker

Open WebUI was built for people who want to explore AI — browse models, tweak parameters, plug in different backends. That's powerful if you're an AI enthusiast, but it's noise if you just need your agent to work.

Frontier gives you a clean, focused workspace. You log in, you see your project, you see the agents built for your team. No model dropdowns. No configuration panels. No wondering which option is the "right" one. Just the tools you need, ready to go.

## An Interface Built Around Your Work, Not a Chat Box

Open WebUI gives every user the same fixed chat window. It doesn't matter if you're in finance, operations, or customer support — the interface is identical, and it can't be changed. There's no way for your team or your admin to reshape the experience. What you see is what everyone sees.

Frontier's **Site Builder** changes that entirely. Your admin can design a **custom workspace** tailored to your team — with structured forms for input, live data tables, embedded chat windows wired to the right agent, and pages organized around your actual workflow. Need an intake form that feeds directly into your agent? A dashboard that surfaces results alongside a chat panel? A different layout for each department? Frontier makes that possible. Open WebUI doesn't — it's a chat window, and a chat window is all it will ever be.

The difference is fundamental: Open WebUI locks you into a generic interface. Frontier lets the interface adapt to the job.

## Stability You Can Count On

Here's something you'll never see, and that's the point: every change to your agent — a prompt update, a configuration tweak, a model swap — goes through a **formal approval process** before it reaches you. Nothing changes silently overnight.

And if something does go wrong? Your admin can **roll back to a previous version** in seconds. You keep working without interruption.

Open WebUI has no approval workflow and no version history. A change is live the moment someone hits save. For a personal tool, that's fine. For something your team depends on daily, it's a risk you don't need to take.

## You See Only What's Relevant to You

Not every agent is meant for every person. In Frontier, permissions are granular — your admin controls exactly which agents your team can access, down to the individual user or security group level. You see what's relevant. Nothing more, nothing less.

Open WebUI's access control operates at the model level, not the agent level. In a shared deployment, you're more likely to encounter agents, models, or configurations that weren't meant for you.

## Enterprise-Grade Security by Default

Frontier integrates with your organization's existing identity infrastructure — LDAP, Active Directory groups, role-based access control. Your login, your permissions, and your data boundaries are governed by the same systems that manage everything else in your enterprise.

Open WebUI's authentication was designed for smaller, self-hosted setups. It works, but it wasn't built with enterprise identity management as a first principle.

## So Why Does This Matter?

Both platforms connect you to the same agent. But the experience of *using* that agent is fundamentally different.

| | **Frontier** | **Open WebUI** |
|---|---|---|
| **What you see** | Your project, your agents | A model picker with everything visible |
| **Interface** | Fully customizable dashboards, forms, tables, pages | Fixed chat window — no customization possible |
| **When things change** | Approved and versioned before it reaches you | Live immediately, no review gate |
| **Access control** | Per-agent, per-user, per-group | Per-model, broad access |
| **Enterprise auth** | LDAP, AD groups, RBAC built-in | Basic user management |
| **If something breaks** | Instant rollback to previous version | No version history |

## The Bottom Line

Open WebUI is a great tool for exploring AI. Frontier is a great tool for **using** AI — reliably, securely, and in a way that's shaped around how your team actually works.

You shouldn't have to think about the platform. You should just open it, and it works.

**Choose Frontier.**
